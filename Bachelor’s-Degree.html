📖 Complete Course Catalog

Explore our comprehensive curriculum, designed in consultation with industry leaders.

FIN101: Principles of Finance • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Introduction to the principles of finance, including the time value of money, valuation of securities, capital budgeting, and risk-return tradeoffs. Students learn to analyze financial decisions using quantitative tools and frameworks.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Apply time value of money concepts to valuation problems
  • Evaluate investment opportunities using NPV, IRR, and payback
  • Understand the relationship between risk and return
  • Value stocks and bonds using appropriate models

Assessment: Quizzes (20%), Midterm Exam (30%), Final Project (25%), Final Exam (25%)

ACC101: Financial Accounting • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Fundamentals of financial accounting, including the accounting cycle, preparation of financial statements, and interpretation of accounting information for decision-making.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Prepare and interpret balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow statements
  • Understand accounting principles and standards
  • Analyze financial statements using ratio analysis
  • Identify ethical issues in financial reporting

ECO101: Microeconomics • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Study of individual economic agents, including consumer behavior, producer theory, market structures, and the role of prices in allocating resources.

MTH105: Mathematics for Finance • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: None ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Mathematical foundations essential for finance, including calculus, linear algebra, probability theory, and optimization techniques.

FIN201: Investments • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: FIN101 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Analysis of investment alternatives, including stocks, bonds, and derivatives. Portfolio theory, asset pricing models, and performance evaluation.

FIN210: Financial Modeling • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Core Course 📊 Prerequisite: FIN101, ACC101 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Hands-on course in building financial models using Excel. Students construct three-statement models, valuation models (DCF, LBO), and scenario analysis tools.

FIN301: Advanced Corporate Finance • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Advanced Course 📊 Prerequisite: FIN202 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: In-depth analysis of complex corporate finance topics, including mergers and acquisitions, leveraged buyouts, private equity, and corporate restructuring. Case-study based.

FIN401: Investment Banking • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Capstone Elective 📊 Prerequisite: FIN301 ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Practical introduction to investment banking, including deal sourcing, pitch book preparation, valuation techniques, and transaction execution. Guest lectures from industry professionals.

FIN490: Capstone Investment Project • 3 Credits

▼
📚 Capstone 📊 Prerequisite: Completion of all core courses ⏱️ 45 Contact Hours

Course Description: Culminating experience where students manage a real investment portfolio, present investment recommendations, and defend their strategies to a panel of industry experts.
