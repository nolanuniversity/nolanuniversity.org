About the Lewis School

Why we exist and what makes us different

Most universities teach finance in one building and entrepreneurship in another. Students graduate knowing one side of the table — but not the other.

The Lewis School of Entrepreneurial Finance was created to close that gap. We are the first degree-granting institution dedicated entirely to the discipline of funding innovation.

Our Mission: To educate and empower a new generation of financial leaders who think like founders and founders who think like financiers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes the Lewis School different from a traditional finance degree?
Traditional finance degrees prepare you for careers in banking or corporate finance. The Lewis School focuses specifically on entrepreneurial finance — funding new and growing ventures. You will learn startup valuation, term sheet negotiation, and investor pitching, not just corporate financial reporting.
Do I need a background in finance to apply?
Undergraduate programmes: No. All required finance foundations are taught within the degree.

Postgraduate programmes: Introductory finance knowledge is recommended. A pre-course module is available.

Executive certificates: No prior finance required.
Can I take courses in other Nolan University schools?
Yes. Lewis School students can cross-register for electives in the School of Entrepreneurship & Venture Creation, School of Technology & AI, and School of Global Business & Leadership.
What is the Lewis Venture Fund?
A student-managed micro-fund that deploys real capital into early-stage ventures. Students serve as analysts, associates, and fund managers — gaining hands-on experience in deal sourcing, due diligence, and investment decision-making.
What scholarships are available?
Founder's Scholarship (full tuition), Mwaba Lewis Diversity Scholarship, Merit Scholarship (up to 50%), and Need-Based Grants. Visit our Financial Aid page for details.
